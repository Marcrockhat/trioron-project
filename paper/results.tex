\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\S\ref{sec:headline} establishes the headline: manifold replay at 30~KB
beats fixed-EWC $\sigma$-confidently at matched parameters.
\S\ref{sec:competitor-sweep} sweeps five continual-learning families
against the same protocol. \S\ref{sec:pareto} traces the
storage-vs-accuracy Pareto. \S\ref{sec:archive-results} reports the
dream-archive Phase~1 $+$ Phase~2 storage win.
\S\ref{sec:extension-results} walks through the ship-wake-extend loop
end-to-end at 23 tasks.

\subsection{Manifold Replay (Headline)}
\label{sec:headline}

Table~\ref{tab:trioron-panel} reports the $n = 3$ multi-seed panel on
chained-15 for the four primary trioron arms with manifold replay enabled.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{chained-15, $n = 3$ trioron panel. \headline\ marks the headline arm.}
  \label{tab:trioron-panel}
  \begin{tabular}{lccc}
    \toprule
    arm                              & full              & domain            & task              \\
    \midrule
    \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small}          & $0.585 \pm 0.002$ & $0.655 \pm 0.006$ & $0.951 \pm 0.001$ \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_no\_dream}   & $0.577 \pm 0.011$ & $0.661 \pm 0.009$ & $0.951 \pm 0.000$ \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_dream}       & $0.587 \pm 0.008$ & $0.657 \pm 0.009$ & $0.956 \pm 0.003$ \\
    \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}     & $0.604 \pm 0.003$ & $0.679 \pm 0.010$ & $0.960 \pm 0.001$\,\headline \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The headline arm (\arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}) reaches $0.604$~full /
$0.679$~domain / $0.960$~task at 30~KB of manifold replay storage.
Forgetting is negative across all three seeds ($-0.566 \pm 0.009$ on
\arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}): past-class accuracy \emph{increases} over
time after a task ends. The manifold replay block exposes the network to
past classes throughout the curriculum, so old-class decision boundaries
continue sharpening even after the task is over --- qualitatively new
behavior relative to any prior rehearsal mechanism, which at best minimize
forgetting toward zero.

Paired $\sigma$-comparisons (Table~\ref{tab:trioron-paired}) confirm that
growth $+$ manifold replay $\sigma$-confidently beats fixed-EWC at matched
trainable parameters.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Paired comparisons, $n = 3$ paired by seed.}
  \label{tab:trioron-paired}
  \begin{tabular}{llrr}
    \toprule
    comparison                                                                  & metric  & mean $\Delta$ & $\sigma$    \\
    \midrule
    \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small} vs \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}                     & full    & $-0.018$      & $-6.36$     \\
    \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small} vs \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}                     & task    & $-0.009$      & $-4.08$     \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_no\_dream} vs \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}              & full    & $-0.027$      & $-3.44$     \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_no\_dream} vs \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}              & domain  & $-0.018$      & $-18.14$    \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The $+6.4\sigma$ paired margin against \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small} at
full-softmax is the strongest growth-vs-fixed result we have measured in
this project. Earlier benches in a regression arena showed
growth-vs-fixed advantages of the same sign but only $+1.0$--$1.2\sigma$;
manifold replay is the variance-tightening ingredient that lifts a
borderline central-tendency win to a $\sigma$-robust one.

Manifold-grown $\sigma$-dominates every named competitor on full-softmax

  \label{tab:paired-sigma}

    vs Online EWC           & $+0.417$ ($+13\sigma$)   & $+0.332$ ($+14\sigma$)     & $+0.002$ ($+1.6\sigma$)  \\
    vs LwF $+$ EWC          & $+0.405$ ($+8\sigma$)    & $+0.298$ ($+6\sigma$)      & $-0.005$ ($-26\sigma$)\,\headline \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

LwF $+$ EWC marginally beats manifold-grown on task-aware accuracy by
$0.005$ absolute ($-26\sigma$ on a tight std). The mechanism is
structural: LwF distills the live network's logits toward the anchored
network's logits on old-class columns, which directly optimizes the
within-old-class discrimination that task-aware measures. We name this
loss explicitly. On the two harder metrics --- full-softmax (no task ID at
inference) and domain-aware --- manifold-grown wins by $+6$ to
$+47\sigma$.

The pattern is consistent with earlier regression-arena results: every
borderline central-tendency win in the regression arena (grown vs
PackNet, vs HAT, vs Online EWC, vs fixed-EWC) widens to a
$\sigma$-confident classification-arena win once manifold replay is
added. Two interpretations are consistent with the data: (a) manifold
replay is the variance-tightening ingredient that lifts central-tendency
wins to robust ones; (b) classification with informative Fisher
(cross-entropy) is a fairer arena for EWC-family methods than regression
with hinge-contrastive (where Fisher saturates near zero) --- and yet
manifold-grown still dominates.

\subsection{Storage-vs-Accuracy Pareto}
\label{sec:pareto}

Manifold replay's 30~KB sketch is competitive with episodic-exemplar
buffers an order of magnitude larger.
Table~\ref{tab:pareto} sweeps $K$ (samples per class stored) for the
hippocampal-buffer baseline alongside the manifold sketch. All rows
report \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream} as the trioron arm.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Storage-vs-accuracy Pareto ($n = 3$ means).}
  \label{tab:pareto}
  \begin{tabular}{lrrr}
    \toprule
    variant                          & storage   & full    & gap to $K = 50$ \\
    \midrule
    engram $+$ diff (no rehearsal)   & 24~KB     & $0.260$ & $-0.39$         \\
    manifold $(\mu, \sigma)$         & 30~KB     & $0.604$ & $-0.04$\,\headline \\
    combined $+$ hippo $K = 10$      & 174~KB    & $0.570$ & $-0.08$         \\
    combined $+$ hippo $K = 20$      & 324~KB    & $0.601$ & $-0.05$         \\
    hippo $K = 50$                   & 750~KB    & $0.646$ & ---             \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Manifold Pareto-dominates hippo $K = 10$ (cheaper \emph{and} more
accurate), matches hippo $K = 20$ at one-tenth the storage, and lands
within $4$ absolute points of $K = 50$ at one-twenty-fifth the storage.
On task-aware the gap to $K = 50$ is $< 1\%$.

Logit-inversion variants (one-shot DeepInversion, refresh-all, and
diversity-regularized) all peaked at $0.27$ full --- the same
engram-attractor pathology in $128$-dimensional L0 space. Per-class
statistics live on the real L0 manifold by construction; inversion
attractors do not. An ablation removing per-class $\sigma$ ($\mu$-only
replay) loses $\approx 0.07$ absolute on full-softmax at $30$ classes ---
roughly $12\%$ of manifold replay's advantage --- confirming that
per-class noise scale is load-bearing.

\subsection{Dream Archive Phase 1 + Phase 2}
\label{sec:archive-results}

Phase~1 (mid-curriculum row archive) and Phase~2 (end-of-curriculum int8
quantization of archived rows $+$ BF16 active path) are evaluated as a
deployment-side compression layer atop the manifold-grown architecture.
Table~\ref{tab:archive-acc} reports $n = 3$ numbers on the four-arm panel
with archive enabled, paired against the no-archive manifold baseline at
matched seeds.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{chained-15 with archive Phase~1 $+$ Phase~2, $n = 3$.}
  \label{tab:archive-acc}
  \begin{tabular}{lccc}
    \toprule
    arm                              & full v2           & full $\Delta$ vs no-archive & task $\Delta$ \\
    \midrule
    \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small}          & $0.572 \pm 0.004$ & $-0.013$                    & $+0.001$      \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_no\_dream}   & $0.559 \pm 0.004$ & $-0.019$                    & $-0.003$      \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_dream}       & $0.572 \pm 0.009$ & $-0.015$                    & $+0.006$      \\
    \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}     & $0.617 \pm 0.004$ & $+0.013$                    & $+0.006$      \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Phase~1's training-time effect lies inside seed-to-seed variance: the same
code on the same seeds produced \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}
$\Delta = +0.013$ in run~v2 versus $-0.004$ in run~v1, attributable to
torch nondeterminism in archive-trigger timing (task-1 accuracies already
differ before any archive fires). We cannot $\sigma$-confidently claim
Phase~1 helps or hurts during training. Phase~2 (int8 snap of archived
rows $+$ BF16 active path) is \textbf{lossless within $\Delta \leq 0.0008$}
across every arm and every seed. The BF16 $+$ int8 mixed precision
matches FP32 $+$ int8 within $\Delta \leq 0.0008$ as well.

The substantive win is on storage. Table~\ref{tab:archive-storage}
reports BF16-baseline deployment storage with and without the archive
$+$ int8 path.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Deployment storage, BF16 baseline, $n = 3$ means.}
  \label{tab:archive-storage}
  \begin{tabular}{lrrr}
    \toprule
    arm                              & BF16 baseline & BF16 $+$ int8 archived & $\Delta$        \\
    \midrule
    \arm{fixed\_ewc\_small}          & $213.7$~KB    & $130.4$~KB             & $-39\%$         \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_no\_dream}   & $213.7$~KB    & $128.9$~KB             & $-40\%$         \\
    \arm{grown\_capped\_dream}       & $211.2$~KB    & $127.5$~KB             & $-40\%$\,\headline \\
    \arm{grown\_uncapped\_dream}     & $224.9$~KB    & $137.2$~KB             & $-39\%$         \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Adding the 30~KB manifold buffer, the headline \arm{grown\_capped\_dream}
deployment image is \textbf{$157$~KB total} (vs $241$~KB BF16-baseline
$+$ manifold, $-35\%$). At an FP32 deployment baseline the same arm lands
at $168$~KB FP32 $+$ int8 ($-63\%$ relative), but the FP32 framing is the
wrong yardstick for a device-conscience deployment; we report BF16
throughout this work because BF16 is the inference dtype.

The $-0.015$ full-softmax cost on \arm{grown\_capped\_dream} comes from
L1 archive, not L0: by task~15 the archive has typically locked $32$--$49$
of L1's $48$--$92$ rows, and rows locked mid-curriculum cannot adapt to
later tasks. L0 archive is functionally a no-op (L0 is already frozen at
init); locking L0 only enables the Phase~2 quantization, where most of
the storage saving lives. Future work: more conservative L1 trigger
(higher streak threshold, lower \textit{archive\_max\_per\_layer}), or
skip L1 archive entirely and rely on L0 quantization alone.

\subsection{Extension: Ship-Wake-Extend Loop}
\label{sec:extension-results}

We validate the full deployment loop end-to-end on a chained-15 $\to$
consolidation-dream $\to$ $+8$ EMNIST K..R curriculum (1 seed, 4
epochs/task, \arm{grown\_capped\_dream}).

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Architecture progression across the ship-wake-extend loop.}
  \label{tab:extension}
  \begin{tabular}{lcrcr}
    \toprule
    phase                & layers          & trainable    & archived             & plastic       \\
    \midrule
    start                & $(128, 32, 2)$  & $4\,194$     & $[0,\,0,\,0]$        & $4\,194$      \\
    chained-15 done      & $(128, 48, 30)$ & $7\,662$     & $[104,\,32,\,0]$     & $3\,534$      \\
    chained-23 done      & $(128, 80, 46)$ & $14\,046$    & $[128,\,40,\,0]$     & $8\,886$      \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The plastic envelope more than doubles during extension
($3\,534 \to 8\,886$ trainable parameters), allocated against the new task
budget --- but the archived rows from chained-15 are honored throughout.
The total stored network grows by only $2.5\times$ while task coverage
grows from $30$ to $46$ classes.

\paragraph{Accuracy at 23 tasks.}
Full $= 0.521$, domain $= 0.693$, task $= 0.948$. Per-phase task-aware
accuracy: MNIST $0.970$, Fashion $0.982$, EMNIST $0.927$ --- original
tasks survive through the extension at task-aware $\geq 0.93$.
Full-softmax dropped from $0.572$ (chained-15-only) to $0.521$
(chained-23): partly mechanical ($46$ vs $30$ argmax competitors per
query, every sample now has more wrong answers to choose from), partly
real MNIST drift from extension-time growth (per-phase MNIST full
$0.752 \to 0.678$). The $-0.014$ task-aware drop is small and is the
right metric for trioron's deployment role of context-gated
personalization atop a frozen LLM.

\paragraph{Deployment storage} (BF16 baseline).
$121.3$~KB network (L0 $= 98.8$, L1 $= 15.3$, head $= 7.3$) plus
$\approx\!47$~KB manifold buffer ($46$ classes $\times$ $256$ floats)
$\approx$ \textbf{$168$~KB total} --- $+11$~KB over the chained-15
deployment for $8$ additional task capabilities.

The verdict: a $157$~KB chained-15 model can be shipped, woken on K..R
data in the field, allowed to grow plastic substrate ($16 \to 40$ plastic
L1 rows), and re-shipped at $168$~KB with original tasks preserved at
task-aware $\geq 0.97$. The architecture supports continual learning
\emph{across deployment cycles}, not only across task boundaries within a
single training run.

\paragraph{Limits we are honest about.}
Full-softmax on this cohort is data-bounded. Per-dataset MLP joint-training
upper bounds are MNIST $\approx 98\%$, Fashion-MNIST $\approx 92$--$93\%$,
EMNIST-letters $\approx 91$--$92\%$; the combined 46-class weighted ceiling
is $\approx 0.927$. Genuine class confusions (T-shirt / shirt / pullover;
I / L; O / Q; B / 8) make $99.9\%$ full-softmax unreachable on this
benchmark at any parameter count under an MLP head, and we do not promise
it. The defensible claim is: \textbf{$0.95+$ task-aware on $23$
class-incremental tasks at $168$~KB total deployment, with on-device
continual learning capability across deployment cycles} --- a regime no
existing method occupies.
